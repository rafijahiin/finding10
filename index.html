Understanding Finding 10: The Core Argument

Finding 10 states: UNFPA has made a substantial but uneven contribution to strengthening national and district systems, shifting from projectized delivery to routine, integrated public services.

Evidence of Substantial:
  • • 79% MMR reduction (2000-2023)
  • • 73% facilities with quality midwifery
  • • Codified standards and SOPs
  • • DHIS2 integration advancing
Evidence of Uneven:
  • • Child marriage: 51.4% before 18
  • • Regional disparities (31%-67%)
  • • Adolescent fertility stagnant
  • • Disability data incomplete

1. Maternal Mortality: Strong Systems Contribution

MMR Trajectory 1990-2023

WHO/UNICEF/UNFPA/World Bank 2025
Key Evidence:
  • • 574 (1990) to 115 (2023) = 79% reduction
  • • Exceeded global 40% average
  • • Annual reduction: 7% (2000-2023)
  • • ~4,000 maternal deaths saved (2023)

Why Substantial: Direct UNFPA

  • • 73% facilities with quality midwifery (UNFPA 2024) - target exceeded
  • • 30% hospitals with GBV training (UNFPA 2022-24) - targets met
  • • MISP in national plans - policy-mandated SRHR
  • • SOPs for GBV - standardized care

Why Uneven: Constraints

  • • Rural-urban: 30% home births
  • • Wealth: 18% vs 74% skilled birth
  • • District variation in ANC 4+
  • • Stock-outs persist
